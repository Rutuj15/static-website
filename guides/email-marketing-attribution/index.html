A Comprehensive Guide to Email Marketing Attribution

ByManpreet Kaur

25 Oct, 2024

5 mins read

Imagine you run an online store and send three emails to a customer: email 1 is a welcome email, email 2 promotes your products, and email 3 is a reminder email about the promotion. The customer opens all three emails on the day you send them and browses your website, and buys something from you on the day he opens the third email.

Which email do you think deserves the credit for this conversion?

This is where email marketing attribution comes into play. It helps businesses determine which email contributed to specific customer actions.

In this guide, weâ€™ll discuss what email marketing attribution is, its different models, and how to set it up.

What is email marketing attribution?

Email marketing attribution is the process of identifying and analyzing which email or series of emails worked to achieve a specific goal, such as increasing sales, acquiring new subscribers, or driving traffic to a website. It helps businesses understand which emails work most effectively to convince customers to take action.

Why does email marketing attribution matter for email marketers?

Email marketing attribution allows email marketers to measure the effectiveness of their email marketing campaigns. It helps them track how specific email campaigns contributed to sales and revenue goals. By assigning credit to various touchpoints, marketers can find which emails drive conversion and identify the ones that need improvement.

Further, it can help them to optimize their upcoming content and strategies based on these insights. By knowing which content or subject lines led to better engagement, they can improve their email campaigns and target prospects.

Types of email marketing attribution models

There can be multiple types of email marketing attribution models based on the goal that youâ€™ve set for your email or email campaign. Here are the main types of email marketing attribution models:

1. Email open attribution: Email open attribution model gives credits to emails when they are opened by the recipients.

2. Click-based attribution: A click-based attribution model assigns credits to an email when the recipient clicks on the links or other CTAs within the email.

3. Custom attribution model: A custom email marketing attribution model is a personalized way to understand how different emails lead to conversions. It aligns with specific needs and goals of a business allowing marketers to assign value to each email based on the customer journey.

How to build a custom attribution model for your business

If you want to create a custom attribution model for our business, start by analyzing and assigning values to different touchpoints. For example, you might prioritize touchpoints closer to the conversion, such as a free trial sign-up. Identify the metrics and criteria by understanding marketing channels that you use and tracking their performance.

To decide on touchpoints and metrics, consider these questions:

  • What subscriber actions do you want to increase?

  • Are you focusing on a specific timeframe for conversions?

  • Do you have repeat customers? How do they engage?

  • Are there any engagement goals related to revenue?

Conclusion

Email marketing attribution makes it easy to understand which email campaigns are driving the most value. By using attribution models, marketers can identify which emails and interactions lead to sales or conversions, helping them make data-driven decisions and run more effective campaigns. By tracking metrics and making changes based on real-time data, you can ensure your email marketing efforts are not just reaching inboxes but also helping to increase your revenue.

FAQs

For email marketing attribution, you can use tools such as Mailmodo, Google Analytics, HubSpot, Mailchimp, and ActiveCampaign.

The metrics you should track for email marketing attribution depend on your goals and requirements. For instance, if your objective is to increase website visits, focusing on metrics like click-through rates will be more valuable than tracking email open rates.

The key metrics for evaluating the performance of email marketing campaigns are: Open rates Click-through rates (CTR) Conversion rates Total revenue generated from specific campaigns.

To choose the right attribution mode start by considering your target audience and how they engage with your email service provider (ESP). Assess the complexity of your marketing efforts as well and experiment with different models to determine the one that gives valuable insights for your business.
