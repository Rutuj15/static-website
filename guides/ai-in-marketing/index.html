Will AI take over digital marketing?

Humans seem to be falling prey to the success of AI tools, or so it seems. Will machines become more intelligent than humans?

1. Lack of originality

While AI tools can analyze all the data on the internet, they can only provide you with what is already available. Due to its inability to yet infuse meaning into its creations, AI is unable to engage in independent creativity.

It is limited by the algorithms and training data used to generate it, so you canâ€™t expect it to exhibit social intelligence.

2. Inability to separate the truths from the lies

AI relies on whatever is available over the internet. If there is a statement that has been posted several times without any background check, AI does not disregard it. It takes it as true and produces the same information for the person whoâ€™s requested it.

3. Data privacy concerns

The world has seen a rise in the digitalization of data. Almost all the information about you can be found online. Companies with millions of customers store thousands of terabytes of data, including information from something as general as their age to something as sensitive as their credit card details.

Giving AI access to such data surely exposes the personal information of millions of users worldwide. Thus, it seems unlikely that the human touch can be eliminated completely from marketing.

So, let's just say that it isnâ€™t AI vs. humans, but AI for the humans in this AI marketing industry.
