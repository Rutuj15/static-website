Campaign Monitor Review: Is This an Ideal Email Marketing Software?

ByNupur Mittal

11 Nov, 2024

14 mins read

Campaign Monitor is an amazing email service provider with top-notch features and an easy-to-use interface. From designing beautiful and engaging emails to setting up automated journeys, you get everything in this tool.

But is this tool really as good as it claims to be? I dug deep into its features by analyzing the tool personally and also took into consideration its customers' feedback. The information in this article results from our extensive research to help you make a rational decision.

What is Campaign Monitor?

Campaign Monitor is a full-fledged email marketing software that helps you create and automate email campaigns, build signup forms, and manage all campaigns in a single dashboard. The software is known for its minimal design and straightforward interface, suitable for email marketing beginners, solopreneurs, and agencies.

Recently they also released SMS campaigns which are available only to send SMS to US-based customers. You can personalize each SMS based on the receiver's traits, such as name, age, gender, and other relevant details.

What are Campaign Monitor's core features?

Here's a list of some of Campaign Monitor's features:

Drag-and-drop email template builder

When creating your first email, you have three options: use a premade template, import your HTML template, or use a plain text email. If you select the 'use a template' option, you'll see 100+ premade templates and 4-premade layout blocks. You can choose either one of them to start creating your first email.

What did I like about this feature?

  • All premade templates were creative and could be used with minimal customizations

  • Preview option that shows how the template will look on mobile and desktop, along with and sender name.
  • Simple and easy-to-navigate UI.
  • Option to check for invalid or broken links.

  • Free in-built stock images from Pexels
  • Easy to set up A/B testing campaigns for two email variations, and you get three options to test - subject line, sender name, or design.

What did I dislike about this feature?

  • Limited premade layout blocks (only four)
  • A/B test only 2 variants of emails.
  • Template error and fix options are not there, which can help identify rendering or formatting errors before sending.

Email automation builder

Email automated journey builder is quite simple, and it offers 6 trigger options: Subscriber joins the list, action in your online store, Subscriber enters or exists a segment, certain date, and an anniversary of a date.

What did I like about this feature?

  • One can easily navigate the automation builder.
  • The left navigation bar makes it easy to add and edit different steps.

What did I dislike about this feature?

  • You can create automation, but you cannot make them live if you're on a free plan. The team doesn't clarify this upfront, and you might make a complete journey but cannot send it.
  • However, if you want to add a new email to a user journey, there is an issue. You'll need to come out of your Campaign and set up the new email separately, which feels a bit clunkier than it needs to be.

Signup form and landing page builder

The signup form builder is an additional feature to create and embed forms on your website. You can see the data collected via these forms in Campaign Monitor's dashboard.

What did I like about this feature?

  • The form builder is easy to use, and you can configure and implement forms without much hassle

What did I dislike about this feature?

  • No option to optimize the form for mobile devices, which is crucial as pop-ups can be introduced if not optimized for mobile devices
  • Limited formatting and styling options are available to create a signup landing page.

Email campaign reporting

Campaign Monitor's email analytics dashboard is one of the best, with advanced reporting and visual representation of campaign performance.

What did I like about this feature?

  • Distinguished sections map out the metrics for the automated journey, contacts, and campaigns.
  • Campaign performance comparison with the previous 30 days, and it shows the change prominently (check the image below)

  • In-depth, visual audience data analysis by subscriber growth numbers, segment, and engagement level. Analyzing and making the required changes to the campaign is really helpful.

What did I dislike about this feature?

  • No option to auto-segment users based on their open or engagement level and send them additional emails.

Campaign Monitor's key strengths and weaknesses

Based on Campaign Monitor's customer review, we have highlighted the pros and cons of the tool:

Campaign Monitor Pros

  • User-friendly interface: The tool has a trouble-free UI; even first-time users can navigate and set up their projects seamlessly.

  • Top-notch analytics: Campaign Monitor stands out among other ESPs with its detailed and visually appealing reporting dashboard.

Campaign Monitor Cons

  • Limited integrations: Despite having 250+ integration Campaign Monitor doesn't have social media integrations with Instagram, and certain CRM softwares.

Campaign Monitor is limited in integrations with business software tools we use in the office. This has left us shortchanged, as there are numerous untapped opportunities we could take advantage of if Campaign Monitor integrates with more software programs. - Reviewer on Gartner Peer Insights

  • Chances of overriding the edits in template builder: The tool doesn't show how many people are working on the same email template, so the chances of overriding others' edits are there.

There is no way to tell when other team members are working on an email Campaign in the email builder. This has been an issue for us on multiple occasions where two people work in an email, and someone's work gets accidentally overridden. There is also no manual save feature; instead, it auto-saves. We've had issues with emails not getting saved correctly due to this. - Becca Sangwin, Marketing Director, The Gottman Institute

Is Campaign Monitor the right tool for you?

Now, the biggest question. Should you use Campaign Monitor as your email service provider? We've jotted down key pointers to help you decide:

When it's recommended

  • If you're a beginner email marketer: Campaign Monitor UI is easy to navigate with its' top navigation panel and clear distinction between different features.

  • If you run an agency: Campaign Monitor is a great tool for agencies as it offers a client management dashboard, in-built billing, and template sharing between sub-accounts.

  • You're looking for excellent email templates: The premade templates are among the best I have ever seen in an ESP. So, if you don't want to start from scratch and customize the existing one, go for Campaign Monitor.

When it's not

  • If you want to run advanced split testing: You can test only two versions of emails by using the subject line, sender name, and design as testing variables. You cannot split test the entire email journey, which you can in Active Campaign.

  • If you want advanced features at an affordable price: Despite all its top-notch features, the Campaign Monitor's pricing is a bit higher than what other ESPs charge for the same features. For instance, you have to pay $29/month to send unlimited emails to only 500 subscribers, while GetResponse charges about half of that to send unlimited emails to a database with 1,000 addresses.

The verdict

Campaign Monitor is one of the best email marketing tools with easy-to-use features such as a drag-and-drop editor, 120+ creative email templates, an automation builder, and an SMS campaign builder. Apart from this, the tool offers two pricing structures: contact-based pricing and pay-per-campaign pricing.

But, despite these features, the tool lacks certain features, such as advanced split testing and assigning tags to contacts. Also, no free trial is available, which can be a major turn-off for beginner email marketers.

FAQs

What is Campaign Monitor best for?

Campaign Monitor is best for beginners and agencies who want to create and send beautiful and compelling emails with a drag-and-drop email editor. Campaign Monitor offers special pricing for agencies and lets them manage their client data in a single dashboard. The automation builder is also easy to use and has segmentation and personalization options.

Can you use Campaign Monitor for free?

You can use Campaign Monitor for free, but you can send up to 5 campaigns, and some features, such as email automation, won't be available to you. The paid plan starts from as low as $9/month.

Does Campaign Monitor have a CRM?

No, Campaign Monitor doesn't have a CRM. But you can integrate it with your CRM tool, including Zoho CRM, Zapier, Salesforce, Podio, Hubspot, and Lexer.
