Elastic Email Review 2024: Is This The Right Tool for Your Business?

ByNupur Mittal

22 Apr, 2024

12 mins read

Elastic Email was established in 2010, and over the years, it has occupied the 6th highest market share among email market platforms. With all the features Elastic Email offers, it seems an affordable tool for beginner email marketers. The software is best known for email delivery services through its API integrations and is often used to send bulk emails.

If you're here, you're probably contemplating whether Elastic Email is the right tool for your business. To help you, we have written this Elastic Email review based on our experience using the tool and analysis of its customers' feedback.

What is Elastic Email?

Elastic Email was founded in 2010 as an email marketing and delivery platform that helps users create, send and analyze their email campaigns. One distinguishing feature is its API integrations that allow users to connect Elastic Email with 15+ apps and websites to fetch data and send targeted automated emails.

What are Elastic Email's core features?

Some of the core features Elastic Email is known for are as follows:

‚Ä¢ Drag-and-drop email editor

Elastic Email template editor has basic and essential features and is suitable for getting the job done. The UI is easy to understand, but the options at the top (tags, preview) aren't that intuitive.

There are limited layout blocks as visible in the image, and customization is also limited. But a good factor is you have 116 pre-made templates, even if you're on a free plan.

‚Ä¢ Landing page builder

The landing page builder looks similar to its template builder, which might sometimes get confusing. The features are limited, but you can create landing pages even if you're on a free plan.

There are also 26 pre-made landing page templates to get started with yours.

‚Ä¢ Form builder

You can also build forms such as sign-up and registration forms using Elastic Email form builder. The editor is quite basic, and there is limited customization you can do, no matter which plans you opt for.

You can customize the fields and change the design of the form to a limited extent. It's useful if you want to create and embed basic forms, but Elastic Email might not be a good choice if you want to build compelling forms.

‚Ä¢ Email automation

The email automation builder is quite simple and easy to use. To start, you need to select one out of these four triggers:

  • Contact joins a list
  • Contact opens an email
  • Contact clicks a link
  • The contact status has been changed

After you add the trigger, you can add an email, delay, condition, or action. You get two condition options: if the recipient has opened the email or a contact's status is changed.

Overall the automation feature is so intuitive that if you're a beginner, you will easily learn how to create a marketing email journey. Still, the feature fails when it comes to triggering options, as those are very limited.

‚Ä¢ Email API

Elastic Email offers RESTful API and SMTP relay integration to decapitalize emails to millions of users. It has API documentation and libraries to help developers set up everything quickly. By setting up webhooks, you'll be able to receive notifications for your email performance.

With its inbound email routing feature, you can anonymize your inbox by automatically forwarding these emails to an email address of your choice.

Elastic Email's key strengths and weaknesses

Based on Elastic Email's customer review, we have highlighted the pros and cons of the tool:

Elastic Email pros

  • User-friendly interface: The tool has a trouble-free UI; even first-time users can seamlessly navigate and set up their campaigns.
  • Send bulk emails with ease: Elastic Email is known for its service to send bulk emails.

One of the best ways to send bulk emails under elastic it's very easy to create HTML under elastic and send emails; they can send one thousand emails, and the software doesn't even get hung.

  • Transactional emails: With Elastic Email API, you can set up transactional emails and send them in bulk.

Sending transactional emails is easy for the company; this software is very beneficial; with cost reduction and increased productivity, our business performance increased by 50 %. - Johana M., Small-Business owner

Elastic Email cons

  • Basic email automation: Advanced email automation is a core feature most users look for in an ESP. Elastic Email falls behind as the triggers are limited and you cannot build complicated email journeys.
  • Bad customer support: Due to the unavailability of live chat and chat support, it might take a long to get your queries resolved.

What I don't like about Elastic Email software is the lack of customer service. There isn't a live chat or phone support option, and the response time can be slow.

Is Elastic Email the right tool for you?

Now, the biggest question. Should you use Elastic Email as your email service provider? We've jotted down key pointers to help you decide:

When it's recommended

  • If you're a beginner email marketer: Elastic Email is a good tool to step your foot in email marketing as you can explore the tool on its free plan if you send 100 emails/day. The cost is low, and you can access almost all its features, including automation, template creator, and email API.
  • If you are a freelancer or small/midsize business: Again, the cost is low, and for a small/midsize business, the tool is affordable and useful to create and analyze campaigns.
  • If you send mass or bulk emails: By integrating your CRM tools with Elastic Email's API, you can trigger bulk emails, one of its best features.

When it's not

  • If marketing automation is vital to your campaigns: Elastic Email's automation isn't that advanced, and you can't access it if you're on a free plan. Besides, the trigger options are also limited.
  • If you want to create engaging and dynamic emails: The template builder is limited to a basic email layout, and you cannot add dynamic email content blocks such as RSS feeds or forms inside the Email.

This is not the best choice for those who want highly customized, dynamic content, design template variants, and high input fields for making the campaign close to the target audience. - Irfanuddin S., Senior Academic Counselor

The verdict

Elastic Email is a good tool for a beginner email marketer as the starting plans are affordable with access to all its features. It's well-known for its email API, using which you can scale your Email send volume. But, the problem that often arises is its low deliverability rate. Based on our research, it was a common concern of Elastic Email users.

So, this might not be a good choice from a deliverability perspective, a non-negotiable feature in an ESP. It will hurt your domain and IP reputation, negatively affecting your email performance.
