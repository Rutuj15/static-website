What Is Email Hosting With 9 Best Email Hosting Providers

ByNupur Mittal

16 Nov, 2024

13 mins read

More than 4 billion people use emails, and this number is expected to rise in the coming years. As a result, it becomes crucial to store and secure user information as a business owner. This is where email hosting comes into play.

Email hosting offers you security, enhances accessibility, builds brand awareness and trust, and so much more. However, you can reap these benefits only with the right hosting providers suitable for your business needs.

This guide will talk about must-have features and the nine best email hosting services you can choose from.

What is email hosting?

Email hosting involves providing a server to manage and store your email communications. This service ensures that your emails are securely stored, accessible, and properly managed.

For instance, if your company uses email hosting, your email address might look like this:

john@mailmodo.com.

In this example, "John" is the user’s name, and "mailmodo.com" is the company’s domain. This setup reflects a professional email address that is managed through email hosting services.

It’s important to note that people often confuse the terms "email domain provider" and "email hosting." Both these terms mean the same and are used interchangeably.

An email domain provider is the service responsible for registering and managing the domain name, such as “yourcompany.com”.

Email hosting is the service that actually manages your email accounts, including sending, receiving, and storing emails.

Why should you opt for email hosting?

Email hosting provides you with a host of benefits. Let's discuss them below.

  1. Professionalism: Email hosting provides you with custom domain names to enhance credibility and trust, projecting a professional image.
  2. Security: It equips the user with robust security measures like email encryption and spam filtering to protect sensitive information and prevent breaches.
  3. Reliability: It offers dedicated services, higher uptime and consistent accessibility for uninterrupted communication.
  4. Scalability: Email domain providers easily accommodate growing email volumes and additional features as your business expands.
  5. Support: Paid plans provide dedicated customer support for quick issue resolution and minimal downtime.
  6. Customization: Email hosting offers control over email accounts and settings, and paid hosting services offer advanced features tailored to your specific email hosting needs.

What is the difference between free and paid email hosting?

When choosing an email hosting provider, you can either choose to go with free or paid email hosting. But how do you choose between them? By learning what you get in each.

Free email hosting

Free email hosting allows you to manage email accounts through webmail (POP) and email client (IMAP). Some free email hosting providers are Google’s Gmail, Yahoo, Microsoft’s Hotmail, etc.

Pros and Cons

✅ Have adequate file storage for small businesses and individuals.

✅ No need to commit or sign a contract.

❌ Weaker security measures.

❌ As an email address without a domain name doesn’t create professionalism, the recipient might doubt your identity or authenticity.

❌ Less cloud storage for big businesses.

❌ It might show you irritating banners or advertisements.

Having paid or professional email hosting can offer many benefits over free plans. Let’s discuss the features of paid email hosting services.

Paid email hosting

If your business has a huge audience size or storage requirements, it is recommended to go for a paid email hosting provider. Such a subscription plan will fit the needs of your growing business and offer you many advantages over free hosting:

✅ A personalized professional-looking domain name like yourname@mailmodo.com. Such email addresses help build brand identity and awareness and enhance your organization’s credibility among users.

✅ More storage space.

✅ Robust antivirus and spam filters.

✅ No advertisements.

✅ IMAP/POP3 protocols.

Types of email hosting

When deciding about email hosting, the first thing to clarify is how you will host your emails. Here are the three ways to choose from:

1. IMAP email hosting

IMAP stands for Internet Message Access Protocol. IMAP email hosting transfers all the emails to your server, but it doesn’t get transferred to all the devices you have logged in to.

You can view, organize, and delete the emails stored on the server and decide which emails you want to transfer. That way, you can save storage across devices. Such email hosting is used by businesses.

2. POP3 email servers

It stands for Post Office Protocol 3 and is among the most common email hosting services. Internet service providers often provide email accounts and Internet services.

With POP3 email hosting, all the incoming emails will get downloaded to your device. It allows you to access them even when you are offline.

The limitation of POP3 is that you can’t access the emails on multiple devices, such as if emails are downloaded on your laptop, you won't be able to access them on your mobile phone.

3. Cloud email hosting

Cloud email hosting is affordable and best suited for small businesses or SMEs. Hosting emails on a cloud server offers many benefits like security, easy access to data, cheap upgrades, and no need to purchase hardware or software, reducing your cost.

Examples of cloud email hosting are G Suite by Gmail and Microsoft Exchange Emails by Microsoft.

In addition to IMAP, POP3, and cloud email hosting, you can also choose between shared and dedicated email hosting.

Shared email hosting

Shared email hosting involves multiple users sharing the same server, making it a cost-effective choice for smaller businesses or individuals. The primary advantages of shared hosting include its affordability and simplicity, as the hosting provider manages most of the server maintenance.

However, shared hosting can lead to performance issues due to limited server resources and potential security vulnerabilities if another user’s account is compromised. Additionally, there is limited customization available, which might not meet the needs of all users.

Dedicated email hosting

Dedicated email hosting provides an exclusive server for a single organization or user, offering enhanced performance and security. The major advantages of dedicated hosting are improved performance due to dedicated resources, better security through customizable measures, and greater flexibility in server configuration.

However, this comes at a higher cost and may require more technical expertise to manage or additional expenses for professional management services.

Ultimately, the choice between shared and dedicated email hosting depends on the specific needs of the organization, balancing factors such as budget, performance requirements, and security considerations.

Factors to consider when choosing an email domain provider

Choosing the right email domain provider is an important decision for your business. Depending on your needs, there are several key features to look for to ensure you get the best service. These features can make a big difference in how well your email works and how secure it is.

1. Security features

When selecting an email domain provider, prioritize robust security measures to protect sensitive information. Look for providers that offer advanced encryption for both emails in transit and at rest, comprehensive anti-malware and anti-virus protections, and two-factor authentication to secure accounts from phishing. Additionally, ensure the provider regularly updates their security protocols and offers reliable backup and recovery options.

2. Easy to access and migrate

You may access your email via different platforms that require different configurations. So, look for an email hosting provider that offers you access to webmail, IMAP, and POP.

  • Webmail - Webmail allows you to use a web-based email client, which is very convenient
  • IMAP - IMAP allows you to read your email from any device without downloading them.
  • POP - POP, on the other hand, requires that you download your emails before reading them.

Assess how easy it is to migrate your existing email accounts and data to the new provider. Look for features or support that facilitate smooth migration with minimal downtime or data loss.

3. Not blacklisted

Having your IP address blacklisted can greatly impact your business activities, and it can take longer to get it non-blacklisted. For that reason, you should avoid email hosts with a reputation for getting domains on blacklists. Why? Because you may end up with a blacklisted recycled IP.

You can check an email host blacklist status on Mx Records.

4. Customer support

Reliable customer support is essential for resolving issues promptly. Look for an email hosting provider that offers 24/7 support through various channels, such as phone, chat, and email. Efficient and knowledgeable support can make a significant difference, especially during critical times.

5. Scalability

Your email hosting provider should be able to accommodate your business growth. Evaluate whether they offer flexible plans or upgrades to handle increasing email volume and additional users as your business expands.

6. Storage capacity

Consider the storage limits for your email accounts and associated files. Ensure that the provider offers ample storage to meet your needs, with options to increase capacity if required.

7. Integration with other tools

If your business relies on other software tools (e.g., CRM systems, marketing platforms, or collaboration tools), check if the email hosting provider offers integrations with these tools. Seamless integration can enhance productivity and streamline workflows.

8. Compliance and data privacy

Ensure that the email hosting provider complies with relevant data protection regulations (such as GDPR, CCPA, etc.) and has measures in place to protect your data. This is particularly important if you handle sensitive or personal information.

9. Pricing

While cost is an important factor, it’s crucial to balance it with the value offered. Evaluate the pricing structure and compare it with the features provided to ensure you’re getting good value for your money.
