Digital Marketing 101: Benefits, Types & Examples

ByMashkoor Alam

19 Jun, 2024

14 mins read

Think of your business as a fantastic restaurant on a side street. You have the most delicious food, but those empty tables will never be filled if no one knows you exist. That's where marketing comes in – it's the neon sign that draws hungry customers through the door.

In today's world, the "door" is the internet. Traditional marketing might be like shouting your menu from that side street – some people might hear you, but many won't. Digital marketing, however, is like strategically placing food photos and glowing reviews online right where potential customers search for their next culinary adventure. It's targeted, efficient, and can turn curious clicks into loyal customers.

In this guide, we'll explore what digital marketing is, the different forms of digital marketing, the benefits it offers, and how to get started on your journey to fill those metaphorical (or literal) tables!

What is digital marketing?

Digital marketing is the umbrella term for all marketing efforts that use the power of the internet. In simple terms, internet marketing leverages online channels to promote products, services, or businesses and reach the target audience for a brand or a company.

It differs from traditional marketing, which uses print media, television and radio. Online marketing uses search engines, social media, email, and websites to connect with current and potential customers. This can involve anything from targeted ads to engaging social media content and even text message marketing.

This further positions your brand precisely where potential customers are actively searching, maximizing your visibility and attracting those most likely to benefit from you.

What are the benefits of digital marketing

Online marketing helps potential customers find you exactly when they're searching for what you offer. By leveraging the power of the internet in the form of digital marketing, your business can drive growth, achieve success and explore untapped opportunities in today's market. The benefits of digital marketing include:

1. Global reach

Traditional marketing methods are often geographically limited. Digital marketing, however, transcends geographical barriers with minimal effort and investment. Anyone with an internet connection can access your website and online presence, allowing you to reach a global audience you might not have been able to connect with otherwise.

Additionally, digital marketing allows for highly targeted campaigns. You can precisely tailor your message and content to specific demographics, interests, and online behaviour, ensuring your message resonates with the most relevant audience segments.

2. Cost-effective

Traditional marketing methods, like television and print ads, often cast a wide net, reaching a large audience with the hope that some percentage might be interested in your product or service. This approach can be expensive and inefficient, with a significant portion of your marketing budget potentially wasted on those with no genuine interest in your offer.

On the other hand, digital marketing offers flexibility when it comes to budgeting. You can see what's working and what's not, allowing you to optimize your strategy to ensure you're getting the most out of your investment. In essence, digital marketing empowers you to spend smarter, not harder. Even small businesses can compete online with the right digital marketing strategies.

3. Measurable results

Unlike throwing flyers out the window and hoping for the best, digital marketing allows you to track and measure the effectiveness of your campaigns in real time. You can track website traffic, clicks, conversions, and engagement metrics with pinpoint accuracy. This data allows you to measure the performance of your campaigns, identify what's working and what's not, and make data-driven decisions to optimize your strategy for maximum impact.

Leveraging analytics allows you to gain deeper insights into user behaviour. These key metrics and measurable outcomes ensure that your digital marketing strategies are bringing in returns for you.

4. Personalization

Digital marketing provides greater access to the needs, preferences and behaviour data. With this knowledge, you can create personalized campaigns that resonate deeply with your target audience. Imagine crafting messages that feel like they were written specifically for each customer, leading to a more positive and engaging experience.

You increase the likelihood of conversions by tailoring your content and offerings to individual needs. Customers who feel understood are more likely to trust your brand and make a purchase.

5. Relationship building

Digital marketing goes beyond simply broadcasting a message. It allows for interactive experiences that capture your audience's attention and leave a lasting impression. Engaging visuals, interactive content formats like polls or quizzes, and interactions on various social media channels all contribute to a more dynamic and engaging marketing experience, building a deeper connection with your target audience.

This interaction with your audience fosters a sense of community and builds stronger customer relationships. You cultivate brand loyalty and encourage repeat business by directly addressing their needs and interests.

B2B vs B2C digital marketing

B2C and B2B digital marketing strategies target different audiences and use other approaches. Choosing between the two methods depends on the nature of your business and intended goals.

Parameter | B2C/DTC | B2B

Target audience | Individual consumers | Other businesses

Content types | Blog posts, videos, social media content, and product demonstrations | Blog posts

Primary marketing channels | Social media, search engines and influencer marketing | LinkedIn and other industry-specific platforms

Purchase decision factors | Emotional factors such as personal preferences and lifestyle | Logical factors such as functionality, features, and cost-effectiveness

Sales cycle | Shorter and impulsive | Longer and involves multiple decision-makers

Types of digital marketing

Just like a well-stocked toolbox empowers you to tackle any project, digital marketing offers a variety of channels to reach your target audience and achieve your business goals. Here's a closer look at some channels you can use to build your brand and reach your customers online!

1. Search engine optimization (SEO)

SEO is the science of optimizing your website to rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs). By optimizing your website with relevant keywords and creating high-quality content, SEO helps search engines understand what your website is about. This allows them to rank your website higher in search results for those keywords. So, when someone searches for something related to your business, your website is more likely to appear near the top of the page, making it easier for potential customers to find you online.

Here's what SEO involves:

  • Search Engines: These are websites like Google where people look for things online (e.g., "best pizza near me").
  • Keywords: These are the words people type into search engines (e.g., "pizza").
  • Relevance: Search engines want to show users the most relevant websites for their searches.

By implementing the following SEO practices, you can increase your website's visibility in search results, attract organic traffic (free website visits from search engines), and ultimately reach more potential customers online.

  • Content: Creating informative, valuable content that uses relevant keywords throughout. This could be blog posts, product descriptions, or service pages.
  • Website Structure: Ensuring your website is well-organized and easy for search engines to navigate.
  • Backlinks: Having other websites link back to yours. This is like getting a good recommendation – it tells search engines your website is trustworthy and relevant.

SEM (search engine marketing) involves techniques like SEO and paid advertising to improve search engine results and brand awareness, increase website visibility, and drive traffic. With SEM, businesses can drive targeted website traffic and increase conversions through strategic ad placement.

2. Content marketing

Content marketing is a crucial aspect of any good digital marketing strategy. This involves creating and sharing valuable content like podcasts, articles, and infographics that educate, entertain, and engage your audience. By consistently providing informative and interesting content, you establish yourself as an authority in your field and attract potential customers who are actively searching for information.

Flipkart's "Stories" section on their website is a prime example of content marketing in action. They publish informative and engaging blog posts that cover various topics, from product reviews and shopping guides to inspiring customer stories and insights into the world of ecommerce.

3. Social media marketing

Social networks like Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn are thriving online communities where you can directly connect with your target audience. Creating engaging social media content, participating in conversations, and running targeted ads can amplify brand awareness among internet users, foster customer relationships, and drive traffic to your website.

Check out Gong's social media presence! They're a prime example of a brand leveraging social media marketing. Their witty and topical posts that often play on current events keep their audience engaged and entertained.

4. Affiliate marketing

Affiliate marketing is a performance-based marketing strategy where businesses reward affiliates for driving traffic or sales to their website through referral links. Affiliates can include bloggers, influencers, or other companies with relevant audiences. By incentivizing partners to promote their products or services, businesses can expand their reach and increase sales without upfront costs.

Strategic affiliate marketing partnerships help businesses tap into larger audiences and generate passive income. This involves partnering with other companies or influencers to promote your products or services. Affiliates earn a commission for every sale they generate through their unique referral link, making it a win-win situation for both parties.

Widening their reach, Nykaa employs an affiliate marketing program. This strategy allows website owners and content creators to promote Nykaa products through unique affiliate links and earn commissions for each sale.

5. Influencer marketing

Influencer marketing involves partnering with individuals or personalities with a large and engaged following on social media to promote products or services. By leveraging influencers' credibility and reach, businesses can tap into new audiences, increase brand awareness, and drive sales through authentic endorsements and collaborations.

Collaborating with social media influencers in your niche can boost your credibility. It also allows your business to connect with your target audience on the platforms they are already engaged in.

Goibibo leverages influencer marketing to reach a wider audience. Take a look at their collaboration with Bollywood actress Deepika Padukone, highlighting the convenience and benefits of using their platform to book travel.

6. Email marketing

Don't underestimate the power of email! Building an email list allows you to send targeted messages directly to potential and existing customers. You can use email marketing to promote new products or services, share valuable content, nurture leads through the sales funnel, and build stronger customer relationships.

Targeted email marketing campaigns can be a highly effective digital marketing approach to reach out to users who have previously shown an interest in your products or services. You can use powerful platforms like Mailmodo to automate content generation, personalize subject lines and develop persuasive call-to-action.

Zomato is a prime example of a company leveraging email marketing effectively. They personalize the customer experience by sending targeted emails with relevant recommendations and special offers, keeping users engaged and driving repeat business.

7. Pay-Per-Click Advertising (PPC)

PPC advertising allows you to target your ideal audience online but only pay when your ad gets clicked. It works across different platforms:

Search Ads: These appear on search engines like Google when users search for terms related to your business. Google Ads is a popular platform for creating and managing your search ads.

  • Display Ads: These banner-like ads appear on various websites, blogs, and apps partnered with advertising platforms. You can target these ads to specific demographics and interests, reaching relevant audiences wherever they browse online.
  • Social Ads: You can create targeted ads displayed directly on Facebook, Instagram, or Twitter. These ads can be tailored to user data like age, location, and even online behaviour, ensuring your message reaches the most relevant people.

By using a combination of these PPC ads, you can strategically target your ideal audience across the web, maximizing your advertising budget and driving valuable clicks to your website.

8. Mobile marketing

Mobile marketing utilizes various tactics to target potential customers directly on their smartphones and tablets. Here's a breakdown of the essential methods:

1. In-App Notifications:

These are targeted messages that appear within apps you're using. For instance, a ridesharing app might send a notification with a discount code for off-peak hours.

2. Phone Notifications:

These are text-based alerts that show up on your phone's home screen, even when you're not actively using an app. News apps might use them to break news, while social media platforms might notify you of new messages. Typically, user permission is required for these notifications.

3. SMS (Short Message Service):

This refers to traditional text messaging used for marketing purposes. Businesses can send SMS messages directly to a customer's phone, like a clothing store sending a birthday discount code or a bank using SMS for two-factor login verification. However, unlike phone notifications, SMS marketing requires customers to opt in beforehand.

By strategically using these mobile marketing techniques, businesses can stay connected with their audience on the go, deliver relevant promotions, and ultimately drive user engagement and conversions.

How to start with digital marketing

Starting your digital marketing journey requires careful planning and strategic execution. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started and achieve success

Define your goals and audience

  • Set SMART goals: Start by establishing clear and measurable objectives for your digital marketing endeavours. These goals should be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, and Time-Bound. Vague goals like "increase brand awareness" lack direction. Instead, aim to "grow our social media following by 20% within the next quarter." This clarity allows you to track progress and measure success.
  • Identify your Ideal Customer: Understanding your target customers is fundamental. Conduct market research to uncover your ideal customer's valuable demographics, interests, and online behaviour patterns. What platforms do they frequently use? What content resonates with them? By creating a buyer persona, you can tailor your digital marketing strategy to speak directly to their needs and preferences.

Choose channels and assets

  • Set a budget: To effectively allocate budget across digital channels, analyzing the potential return on investment (ROI) of different channels is essential. Prioritise channels that resonate most with your target audience while considering their potential for engagement.
  • Select a distribution channel: Digital technologies offer a vast array of channels. Don't try to be everywhere at once! Carefully evaluate which channels align best with your goals and seek expert advice before deciding on the same. Here are some popular choices:
    • Content Marketing: Create valuable blog posts, articles, infographics, or videos that educate, entertain, and engage your audience.
    • Social Media Marketing: Establish a presence on platforms where your target audience resides. Use social media to share content, interact with followers, and build brand awareness.
    • Search Engine Optimization (SEO): Optimize your website and content to rank higher in search results for relevant keywords. This increases organic traffic and attracts potential customers who are actively searching for solutions you offer.
    • Email Marketing: Build an email list and nurture relationships with subscribers by sending targeted email campaigns containing valuable content, special offers, and updates.
    • Pay-Per-Click (PPC) Advertising: Run targeted ads on search engines, social media platforms, or other websites to reach a wider audience and drive traffic to your website or landing pages.
  • Create assets: High-quality and engaging content is the backbone of any effective digital marketing strategy. Focus on creating content that resonates with your target audience, addresses their pain points, and provides them with value. This could include blog posts, articles, infographics, videos, social media content, or email newsletters. Create the content that your target audience would like to consume and share.

Optimise tools, distribution & measurement

  • Choose the right tools to distribute: Choosing the right tools to distribute your content is essential. The tools you use can help you automate repetitive tasks, allowing you to focus on more strategic tasks. Mailmodo, for instance, will enable you to set up scheduled emails to be sent at the most optimal time.
  • Measure the performance: To gauge the effectiveness of your digital marketing efforts, it is essential to measure performance using different tools and metrics like Google Analytics. Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) such as website traffic, conversion rates, engagement metrics, and return on investment (ROI) to evaluate the effectiveness of your strategies.

Embrace continuous improvement

  • Make data-driven decisions: Regularly analyze your performance data. See what's working well and what areas need improvement. Feel free to experiment with different tactics and adjust your strategy based on your findings.
  • Stay Updated: The digital marketing landscape is constantly evolving. Stay updated on the latest trends, algorithms, and best practices to ensure your approach remains effective. Be open to adaptation and evolution as you strive for continuous improvement.
  • Iterate and Improve: Use performance data and feedback to continuously refine and optimize your digital marketing strategies. Experiment with different tactics, messaging, and channels to identify what resonates best with your audience and drives the desired outcomes.
  • Develop a Long-Term Vision: Building a successful digital marketing strategy takes time and dedication. Don't expect overnight results. Be patient, consistent, and focused on your long-term goals.

By following these steps and implementing a well-defined digital marketing strategy, your business can effectively leverage digital channels to reach, engage, and convert your target audience, driving growth and success in the digital age.

Final takeaway

Digital marketing is essential to any modern business, regardless of size or industry. It is the first step for building long-lasting relationships, driving sales, and promoting long-term success by targeting potential customers worldwide. By understanding digital marketing basics, you can develop effective strategies to reach and engage with your target audience no matter where they are.

Building a successful digital marketing presence is a marathon, not a sprint. Be patient, consistent, and focused on your long-term goals. With this guide as your roadmap, you'll be well on your way to being a successful digital marketer.
