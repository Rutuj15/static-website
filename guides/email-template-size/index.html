How to Choose the Right Email Template Size

ByMashkoor Alam

15 Oct, 2024

6 mins read

In email marketing, the template size often influences your campaign's overall effectiveness. A well-optimized template enhances readability and engagement, while an improperly sized one can lead to frustration and disengagement. Having the right email template size is essential for maintaining user interest and ensuring seamless compatibility across devices.

In this article, we’ve gathered insights from industry standards and our extensive client experience to provide you with key guidelines. Learn about the ideal dimensions to use for your email templates to create visually appealing emails that resonate with your audience and improve your marketing efforts.

Why email template size matters

The size of your email template can make or break how well your emails perform. Here’s why it’s critical to get it right:

1. Provides a better user experience

A well-sized email template enhances user experience by making content easy to read and navigate through. This reduces the likelihood of reader frustration and disengagement.

2. Ensures compatibility across devices

Different email clients display emails in their own unique ways, depending on the size of the email templates. So, using the appropriate email template size ensures consistent rendering across various devices and email clients. A consistent experience fosters trust and reliability with your audience.

3. Boosts performance and load times

Larger emails can significantly slow loading times, especially on mobile devices. If your email is bulky and takes too long to appear, users will quickly lose interest. A well-optimized email template will load fast, ensuring that your recipients don’t have to wait to engage with your content.

The ideal email template size

When it comes to ideal email template size, there’s no one-size-fits-all answer; it varies based on your specific design, content and goal. Different email clients render emails in different ways, so the email clients that your audience uses also matter.

Based on industry standards and our experience with clients at Mailmodo, we’ve compiled key guidelines for the ideal email template sizes. Below is a detailed breakdown of the specifications to maximize your email impact.

Width: The Goldilocks zone

The ideal width for most emails sits at 600px. Why? Because it fits like a glove in most email clients; no weird cropping and no scrolling side to side. You can certainly increase the width. Expanding the maximum width to 700px, 800px, or even 900px is also perfectly acceptable. Just make sure your email still renders perfectly across platforms and devices. The last thing you want is a beautifully designed email that no one can fully see and your email campaign getting ruined.

Height: Keep it tight and engaging

There’s no hard-and-fast rule for height, but let’s face it: the longer your email, the faster your readers will drop off. While there’s no limit, less is more when it comes to email template height. One of the best practices is to break your content into digestible chunks, keep it engaging, and be strategic with your CTAs. You’re not writing a novel so, keep the email message concise and make every scroll count.

Weight: Stay under 102 KB

Email weight matters more than you think. If your email exceeds 102 KB, Gmail will clip it, meaning your subscribers will miss out on the full content unless they click “View Entire Message” (spoiler: most won’t). Keep your images compressed, your code clean, and your overall email size light. This ensures faster load times and a better user experience.

Mobile responsiveness: No more pinch and zoom

Here’s the deal: more people are opening emails on mobile than ever before. So, if your emails aren’t optimized for a mobile width of 320px to 480px, you’re asking your audience to pinch and zoom, and nobody likes that. Responsive design techniques, like media queries, ensure your emails adapt to different screen sizes without losing their visual appeal. Keep it sleek, easy to read, and mobile-friendly.

Ideal size for different email template components

When designing an email template, understanding its key components is essential for creating effective communication. Each part plays a distinct role in delivering your message and engaging your audience. In general, the width of the elements majorly depends on the width of the email template. The main elements and their sizes for proper desktop viewing are as follows:

Component Size
Header Width: 600-800px, Height: 100-150px
Content blocks Width: 600-800px, Height: 200-300px each (flexible based on preference)
Footer Width: 600-800px, Height: 100-300px
CTA Width: 150-300px, Height: 50-75px (flexible as per preference)
Images/GIFs Width: 600-800px (depends on use case), Height: Flexible based on need
Email signature Width: 300-600px, Height: 150-200px

1. Header

The email header sits at the top of your email, prominently featuring your logo and branding. A well-designed header email banner should be around 600-800 pixels wide and 100-150 pixels high. A clean header and correct email header size help establish your brand identity while saving space for the rest of your content.

2. Content blocks

Content blocks form the body of your email containing your main message and images. Keep these blocks short and focused, ideally around 200-300 pixels in the total height of your email template. Long blocks can overwhelm readers and lead to lower engagement, so aim for clarity and brevity to enhance the reading experience.

3. Footer

The footer wraps up your email, including essential details like contact information, legal disclaimers, and social media links. An effective footer should be around 100 pixels in height, with a maximum height of 300 pixels. This keeps your email tidy while ensuring your audience has the most important information they need.

💡 Related guide: 8 Inspiring Email Footer Examples in 2024

4. CTA

Call-to-Action (CTA) guides your audience to take specific actions, such as visiting your website or making a purchase. The ideal size for CTA buttons is about 150-300 pixels wide and 50-75 pixels high. On mobile devices, consider making the button full-width to enhance usability. The CTA can be a text too and the font size can follow the normal text size.

5. Image and GIF

Visuals are vital for engaging your audience. The optimal size for email images is around 600-800 pixels wide if you are going for a full-spread image, ensuring clarity across devices. The sizes also depend on the image's use case as you might have multiple images in a row. You can check out our detailed guide on this below. Compress images for faster loading times, aiming for a maximum file size of 200 KB for images and 500 KB for GIFs.

💡 Related guide: What Is the Ideal Email Image Size to Use in 2024

6. Email signature

The email signature is your digital business card. It should include your name, title, company name, contact information, and relevant links (like your website and social media). A well-crafted email signature typically ranges from 300-600 pixels wide and 150-200 pixels high. Keep it professional and consistent with your branding, using easy-to-read fonts and colors.

Bottom line

With these email template size guidelines, you can create emails that grab attention and drive results with your email marketing campaigns. Focus on the right dimensions for headers, content blocks, and CTAs to ensure a sleek look. Prioritize responsiveness and readability to keep your audience engaged. By optimizing your email sizes, you enhance user experience and boost engagement.

FAQs

The ideal width for most email templates is between 600px and 900px. This range fits well within most email clients, preventing issues like awkward cropping or horizontal scrolling that can frustrate readers.

To enhance loading times, focus on optimizing your images by compressing them without losing quality. Keep your email's overall size under 102 KB to get better performance. Also, it is suggested to avoid large attachments.

Marketing emails like transactional email templates generally have simpler, more straightforward layouts that prioritize functionality. In contrast, promotional email templates often feature richer visuals and more email content, resulting in larger overall sizes.

Mobile responsiveness is vital because a growing number of users access emails via mobile and other different devices. Ensuring your emails are responsive prevents the need for pinch-and-zoom, thus improving readability and user engagement significantly.
