AWeber Review 2024: An In-Depth Analysis

ByNupur Mittal

20 May, 2024

14 mins read

AWeber is one of the oldest email marketing platforms. Founded in Pennsylvania in 1998, the ESP serves customers across many verticals and offers essential features to help customers scale their marketing campaigns.

This email marketing tool is known for its email autoresponder and email series builder, email and web analytics, and landing page builder. It also has a Smart Design editor, which helps you custom-create emails based on the URL you feed in it.

But, on the downside, the price-to-feature ratio isn't the most attractive as many features, especially marketing automation, are limited by triggers and conditions.

This AWeber review guide will discuss the core features, pros and cons, and some of the best alternatives you can consider. The review is based on the personal analysis of its features and users' feedback.

What are Aweber's core features?

Here is a detailed analysis of AWeber's key features:

1. Drag-and-drop email template builder

AWeberâ€™s email template editor seems quite basic and not very intuitive. It was difficult to change the email's overall color, font size, and dimension as the editor didn't have a clear option.

Pros:

  • Add and send an interactive AMP carousel. You can embed an interactive AMP carousel inside emails without coding skills.

  • Save time with Smart Designer editor. You can add your website's URL, and the tool will custom-create a template based on the page's assets. It's helpful if you want your email consistent with a specific page on your site.

  • Magic link option. When you add a link to a URL in AWeber's editor, it quickly turns it into a preview of the link's content. It shows the title, featured image, and description of the URL.

Cons:

  • Basic email templates for free plan users: As for the email templates, I didn't find any templates when I clicked on the 'Use one of AWeber's templates.' It redirected me to a blank canvas to create the email. The templates might not be available with the free version.

  • Missing must-have content blocks: The template does not offer premade content blocks such as a navigation menu, email layouts, spacer, etc.

  • Email footer option missing: You can see the email footer in the preview mode, but there is no option to edit or add one in the builder.

New feature: You can now format your emails with AWeber's new markdown feature. It will be most helpful while creating plain text emails.

2. Landing page builder

AWeber helps grow your email list and sell products with its landing page builder To do this, you can create and embed an inline form or pop-up on the landing page.

The tool has 46 creative and ready-to-use landing page templates. The templates are well-designed and might need minimal work to customize.

You can use these templates for various use cases, such as:

  • Sell course subscriptions

  • Promote special offers

  • Consultations and coaching

  • Crowdfunding and donations

  • Grow newsletter subscriptions

  • Get registrations for events and webinars

The landing page builder has the same UI as the drag-and-drop email editor. So it's easy to get familiar with it.

One cool feature is its Smart content option. It picks up content from the URL and helps you add related content assets to your new landing page. Some elements it picks up include -- Facebook posts, Google Maps locations, Shopify product listings, and RSS feeds. This feature can be helpful if you want to recreate an existing landing page. Although, this feature is only available if you're on AWeber's paid plans.

You can also sell products and collect payments by integrating your app with Stripe and Paypal.

3. Signup form builder

You can add a signup form to your landing page or create one for your website. There are basic premade templates that you use to get started, and you can choose from 4 different types of forms:

  • Inline forms

  • Pop-over

  • Pop-up

  • Lightbox

The AWeber's signup form builder for the website seems a bit odd and outdated given all the tools available in the market. The customization options are limited, and if you want to create creative signup forms, AWeber might not be a good choice.

Alternatively, when you create forms for landing pages, you work on the landing page builder. This editor is much easier to use, allowing many options and flexibility to create compelling forms.

4. Email automation builder

You can send one-time autoresponder emails or create a series of emails based on different trigger actions. AWeber has 17 premade email workflows and a marketplace where you can access email sequences of brands like Shopify, Thinkific, etc.

The email automation builder is easy to use, and you can add multiple emails by dragging and dropping. You can add tags to subscribers in your email list to send targeted email series to a segment of subscribers.

One drawback is that the tool doesn't have any if/then conditions to trigger more targeted emails which you can find in other ESPs such as Mailmodo, Mailchimp, and Mailerite. Another thing to remember is that you can create a single automation if you're using AWeber for free.

AWeber's pros and cons

Here are some of the key advantages and disadvantages of using AWeber as your ESP:

AWeber pros

  • Good customer support: With more than two decades of experience, AWeber has been offering reliable support to its customers. It offers a variety of options to talk to its support team via emails, live chat support, and phone calls. You can get access to call support only on paid plans. Besides, if you like to learn independently, you can read their help articles and watch tutorial videos.
  • Compelling landing page templates: AWeber has 40+ creative and ready-to-use templates for different use cases and industries.

AWeber cons

  • Unfair subscriber count and charges: AWeber counts unsubscribers as billable contacts as long as they're in your account. This isn't ideal, as many ESPs like Mailmodo don't charge you extra for keeping an unsubscriber in your AWeber account.

  • Basic email automation builder: Compared to other ESPs such as Mailmodo and Active Campaign, the automation workflow builder only offers a few trigger and segmentation opportunities. For instance, there is only wait and delay condition available.

Is AWeber the right tool for you?

Should you choose AWeber as your email marketing solution? Here are key pointers to help you decide.

When AWeber is recommended:

  • You want instant and reliable support: AWeber's customer service team is reliable and quick to respond to your queries. They also have articles, tutorial videos, and podcasts to familiarize you with their tool. You can access phone call support if you're their paid user.
  • You want to build landing pages: The landing page builder is one of AWeber's best features. You can build compelling pages, embed forms, and use them to promote or sell products and subscriptions. Besides, you can connect it to your custom domain.
  • You want to sell products and services: AWeber integrates with Paypal, allowing you to collect money for your digital products and services on your sales page and via email. Once someone buys from you, you can use this action as a trigger to send relevant and timely emails.

When it's not

  • You want a higher deliverability rate: AWeber might not be a good choice as it didn't perform well in EmailToolTester's deliverability test. Your email campaigns might see the below deliverability rate impacting your domain reputation.

  • You want the best money-to-feature ratio: Given its features, AWeber came across as a less attractive ESP from a feature-to-price ratio perspective. Other ESPs, like Mailmodo, offer even more advanced features at affordable pricing.

  • You want to send interactive AMP emails: AWeber currently offers an AMP carousel that you can embed without any coding knowledge. But, to add other AMP widgets such as polls, forms, and carts, you must code your emails in HTML. In such a scenario, your best bet would be Mailmodo, using which you can create and send interactive AMP emails with its no-code email builder.
