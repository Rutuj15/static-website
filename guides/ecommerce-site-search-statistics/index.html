21 Must-Know eCommerce Site Search Statistics in 2024

BySakshi Chandwani

01 Aug, 2024

4 mins read

In today's tech-driven retail landscape, eCommerce site search isn't just a feature – it's a game-changer. Where every click matters, and user experience is paramount, the role of site search cannot be overstated. For eCommerce businesses, the ability to provide a seamless and efficient search experience can be the key to unlocking customer satisfaction and boosting conversions In this blog post, we're delving into the fascinating world of eCommerce site search statistics.

How do we collect these statistics?

Research methods for gathering eCommerce site search statistics include data analysis that provides accurate and relevant data crucial for informed decisions. Some of the reliable sources are below statistics, including Segmentify, Nosto, and llcbudy.

21 eCommerce site search statistics

Let's have a look at the mentioned points that provide us with unique insights into online search behavior, on-site search experiences, and the impact on customer retention and conversion for e-commerce websites.

  1. 87% of consumers start their product searches online. [Source: Segmentify]
  2. When asked where they typically start their journeys, 56% of customers cited more than one answer between Amazon, a search engine, and a brand’s site. [Source: Segmentify]
  3. 43% of retail customers head directly to the search bar on a website. [Source: Segmentify]
  4. On average, a third of visitors will perform an on-site search. [Source: Segmentify]
  5. On-site search is used more frequently than the navigation menu (49%), filter feature (37%), and homepage recommendations (30%), with 78%. [Source: Segmentify]
  6. 34% of customers try to search for non-product content, such as “returns” or “order tracking”, and 39% of websites don’t support these non-product search queries. [Source: Segmentify]
  7. 42% of all sites perform below acceptable on-site search UX performance across the eight eCommerce search query types. [Source: Segmentify]
  8. As many as 68% of customers would not return to a site with a poor search experience. [Source: Segmentify]
  9. 78% of consumers say the on-site search function on retail websites sometimes provides irrelevant items.[Source: Segmentify]
  10. On-site search users who successfully found what they were looking for led to nearly doubled conversion rates. [Source: Segmentify]

FAQs

Best practices for optimizing site search on an eCommerce website include using autocomplete to suggest popular search terms and products, implementing filters to help customers narrow down their search results, analyzing site search data to identify popular searches and optimize product placement, and using natural language processing to improve search result accuracy.

Utilizing effective site search on an eCommerce website can lead to increased conversion rates. Studies have shown that visitors who use site search are 2-3 times more likely to make a purchase. A well-designed site search improves user experience and helps customers find what they need quickly. Features like autocomplete suggestions and filtering options further enhance the effectiveness of site search.

Mobile and desktop users show differences in site search behavior. Mobile users tend to utilize site search more frequently and are more likely to use voice search. On the other hand, desktop users use more specific keywords in their queries. eCommerce sites should optimize site search for both mobile and desktop users.
