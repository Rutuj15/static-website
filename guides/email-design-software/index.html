12 Best Email Design Software in 2024

ByJyothiikaa Moorthy

31 Oct, 2024

13 mins read

The design of your emails can make or break your email campaign.

A well-designed email can engage readers and get them to click. A poor design, in contrast, can turn off readers and make them bounce off your email.

Misconceptions aside, the design of your email is in your control. All you have to do is choose a good email design software and create beautiful & responsive emails that convert.

We've analyzed the top email design software to help you make the best choice.

Before we discuss the email design software you can use to create your email templates, Know all the basics of email design to understand how to design emails that are beautiful & responsive.

1. Mailmodo

Mailmodo is an all-in-one email marketing software where you can create, design, send, and automate emails to your subscribers using the platform. Mailmodo is best for designing interactive emails with the help of an email template builder which is no-code.

Mailmodo pricing:

You get a 21-day free trial. There are three paid plans presently:

Plan Pricing starts from
Lite $39/month
Pro $79/month
Max $159/month

Type of editor:

Using the WYSIWYG email template editor, you can drag and drop pre-coded content blocks like text, images, and also AMP blocks, etc.

Email templates available:

It has 400+ email templates for different industries and occasions. You can edit and send it out in minutes. You can also import your custom template as an AMP or HTML file.

Export options:

You don't have to export any template you create because you can send the email directly through the email marketing platform.

Responsive:

The designs and templates provided are responsive by default. So you don't have to fuss about emails not rendering well on different devices.

Special email marketing features:

  • You can make your emails interactive by using AMP components like forms, calendars, widgets, etc.
  • You can use the dynamic content to save time and automatically update the content on the emails you have created.
  • You can integrate Mailmodo with APIs and CRM tools like HubSpot, Zapier, Shopify, etc.
  • You can A/B test your subject lines to see which one gets readers to open the email.
  • You can automate and send emails on time using the email automation features.
  • Before sending the email, you can preview what the email looks like on desktop and desktop.
  • You can create and save content blocks and reuse it in other templates.
  • You can also send a test email to yourself or your team members to check how it renders in the email client.
  • You can utilize artificial intelligence to create compelling email subject lines.

2. Hubspot

Hubspot is a CRM platform that helps businesses streamline their marketing, sales, customer service, and content management all in one place.

Hubspot pricing:

In terms of the plans that include email marketing, you have:

Plan Price starts from
Marketing Hub Starter $0/month
Marketing Hub Professional $890/month
Marketing Hub Enterprise $3,600/month

Templates available: They have 5 basic HTML email templates that people can customize with their content and images. They also have several pre-made email templates, but unfortunately, it's not available in the free plan.

  • You can track your leads and how much ROI you have from email marketing for other marketing channels.

What to consider when choosing email design software?

You need to know what you need before you get something; the same applies to choosing an email creation software. Here are some aspects you can look for when choosing one.

  • Responsive design: Does the software allow you to create responsive designs for all devices, especially mobile?
  • Pricing: Do they have a generous free trial/plan where you can try out the software and see if it works for you?
  • Export options: After creating the template, do you have to export it, or can you send the email through the software itself?
  • Templates available: How many sample templates do they provide for you to choose from? Premade templates make it easy for beginner email marketers to create and send campaigns. So best to choose software that provides plenty of pre-made templates.
  • Type of editor: Is it a completely drag-and-drop editor, or does it require some coding knowledge to make adjustments?

For beginners, it's best to choose an email service provider (ESP) like Mailmodo, Mailchimp, etc., as creating an email design system and sending emails all in one platform is easier.

If you are familiar with email marketing and have an easy-to-use ESP, you can check out other exclusive design software like Figma, Topol, etc. But before that, check if your ESP allows custom template import to ensure you can upload the design you create on external software.
