What is transactional email template?

Transactional emails are automated emails triggered when users take action, such as a subscription confirmation email when users opt-in for your emails or an order confirmation email after making the purchase.

These emails are used to inform the recipient about their action or a request made by them, such as password recovery.

How to use transactional emails?

  • Receipts and order confirmation after users make a purchase.
  • Explicit requests made by individuals, such as password reset.
  • Account-related alerts such as payment failed emails.
  • Summaries and weekly digest emails.
  • Subscription confirmation emails.
  • Customer support and feedback request.
  • Emails after account creation.

Why should you use Mailmodo's transactional email templates?

  • Create attractive and engaging transactional emails without coding skills in our easy-to-use drag and drop template editor.
  • Add interactive AMP widgets such as forms, surveys, carts, image carousels, calendars, dropdown menus, etc., in the email.
  • Save time and effort by saving your content design blocks to reuse in the future campaign.
  • Set up a variety of triggers, including API endpoints, Webhooks, and CRM and marketing app integrations.

Each template follows email accessibility guidelines

  • Legible font size and type.
  • Color contrast follows the standard 4.5:1 ratio.
  • Enough white space and smaller paragraphs for better readability.
  • Distinguishable and clear call to action button.
  • ALT text for all the images.

Ensures proper inbox rendering

How to customize and send these templates?

You can customize these templates using our codeless, drag and drop email editor.

  • Pick your template and click on the 'Use this template' button.

  • Mailmodo's email template builder will open. Here, you can customize the template:

    • Try out different email layouts, and add or remove content blocks.
    • Add your email copy, and adjust the heading, subheading, and white spacing.
    • Add interactive elements from our AMP email widget gallery such as forms, surveys, polls, review and rating, carousels, quizzes, etc.

  • Once you make all the changes, preview both the AMP and fallback (HTML) version and check for mobile and desktop previews.

  • Then, save the template.

You can now send the email template to the recipient.
