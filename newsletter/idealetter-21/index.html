#1: Awareness Stage: Introduce your brand

Let’s start with where it all starts — the awareness stage. They have probably heard about your lead magnet or event and signed up for it. If this is their first touch with your brand, you must create a good first impression. 

 

To do that, you need to give them what they have come for - the ebook, a slot in your event, etc. Then, you can introduce your brand and what you do in the same email.

 

In the second email, you can ask for feedback on the resource or event and get to know them better by asking what they find challenging. 

 

Now that you have some data on their interests or struggles, you can slowly introduce your product and how it can help them with their interests or issues in subsequent emails.

 

Here's a prebuilt email automation  flow that you can use for this 👇

#2: Consideration Stage: Nurture the leads

Now that you have introduced yourself and what your brand does, now comes the time to nurture these leads. The goal here is to educate them about your product/service so that they might become customers.

 

To do that, you must establish yourself as the thought leader in the topic/industry. So, send them helpful resources that you have, like ebooks, checklists, newsletters, etc. 

 

You can also send them case studies to provide social proof of your product or service's effectiveness. Lastly, you can offer them a limited-time discount to entice users to sign up and try the product.

 

Here's a prebuilt email automation   flow that you can use for this 👇

#3: Decision Stage: Encourage conversions

Once they have signed up for a free trial and started using your product, next you would want them to convert them into paying customers.

 

To do this, you need to give them a reason to convert. So, send an email introducing some exclusive features only available in the paid plan to show them what they are missing out on.

 

Also, switching to a paid plan is a challenging decision, so make it easy for them to do it by showing some proof of concept. Send an email showing how people use some of your product’s premium features to solve their pain points. 

 

If they haven’t converted, collect feedback to understand their experiences and needs. Then, your team can use this information to contact them to get them to convert.

 

This flow below can be used as a stand-alone for people who directly sign up for a free trial without bypassing the lead stage.

#4: Retention Stage: Adoption & getting repeat business

Adoption:

After they become paying customers, the goal should be to keep them using the product. They are likelier to stick around when they use the product more. 

 

To do this, send them an email showcasing a new or underrated feature that can be helpful for them. If they aren’t trying it out, provide more resources (help articles, blog posts) to help users understand the feature. 

 

You can also add a GIF to showcase how the product/feature works in a few simple steps.

 

Here's a prebuilt email automation  flow that you can use for this 👇

Getting Repeat Business:

Now, from the previous stage, if customers are actively using your product, this can be the time to get them to upsell/cross-sell. 

 

To do this, you can introduce a useful feature/service in a higher-end plan. Explain the benefits they would get from switching to the higher plan, and you can mention some other features included in the plan.

 

If they haven’t upgraded, in the next email, give them an exclusive offer as they are existing customers. Show them some case studies and answer some FAQs to help ease any objections they might have.
